Books:

There are dozens of books on the topic. I have given few of the books I found very useful.

General Books:

  1. Design of Everyday Things (Revised and Expanded Edition) by Don Norman
  2. Designing for Interaction by Dan Saffer
  3. Designing Interaction by Bill Moggridge ( It is the IxD history book)
  4. The User Experience Team of One by Leah Buley
  5. Elements of User Experience by Jesse James Garret
  6. Don’t Make Me Think by Steve Krug
  7. Sketching User Experience by Bill Buxton
  8. About Face 4 by Alan Cooper
  9. Designing for Digital Age by Kid Goodwin

Books on Individual Topics:

  1. Prototyping by Todd Zaki Warfel
  2. Mental Models by Indi Young
  3. Universal Principles of Design by William Lidwell
  4. Designing Interfaces (Revised and Expanded Edition) by Jenifer Tidwell
  5. Observing User Experience A Practitioner’s Guide to User Research by Mike Kuniavsky
  6. Microinteractions by Dan Saffer
  7. Desinging for Emotion by Aaron Walter

Papers and Essays:

  1. Augmenting Human Intellect by Doug Engelbart It is not UX specific and it is very long. But is considered to be most important paper in computer history and you won't regret reading it.
  2. User Interface, A Personal View by Alan Kay
  3. Sketchpad by Ivan Sutherland
  4. Magic Ink by Bret Victor
  5. Experience Prototyping by Marion Buchenau and Jane Fulton Suri

See The IxD Library , which has comprehensive collection of essays and articles. Hope it helps. Happy reading. Happy designing.
